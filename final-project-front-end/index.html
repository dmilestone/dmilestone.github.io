Airbnb Data

An inside look at the data behind listings, reviews, and booking calendars for Airbnb rentals in Asheville, NC.

Click here to check out our data source...

You have to start somewhere...

We found the most relevant data pulled from Inside Airbnb - Adding Data to the Debate were found in three seperate csv files:

  • listings.csv - containing 16 columns and 2441 rows of rental listing data
  • calendar.csv - containing 7 columns and 890,965 rows of booking data
  • reviews.csv - countaing 6 columns and 170,973 rows of reviews matching different listings

So what are we looking at?

Our initial analysis was done in Jupyter Notebook using pandas and matplotlib. First we looked at occupancy over the next year based on the room type of each listing.

Our data has each calendar date over the next year with booking information (price and whether rental is currently booked) for each listing. Since our calendar data starts on 11/29/2019, you can see a clear spike in number of bookings for Thanksgiving and Christmas which were both just around the corner.

Next we looked at the average daily rental price over the next year. With clear spikes in weekend costs, the overall average seems to be trending upward. Are listings more expensive the further out the date?

Hmmm.... The average daily rental price looks like a heart monitor right out of a hospital room. This is due to weekend price flucuations. Let's use the bins method to create a clearer picture.

Ah, there's our story!

After putting our daily prices into bins, a clear story starts to emerge when looking at the number of reviews. It appears renters who spend under $150/night are more likely to leave a review and that likelihood continues to decrease as price goes up. The two variables are negatively correlated, but how much?

Correlation Matrix

We then built a correlation matrix to verify our findings and see what other variables were driving our results.

What!?!?! Accoridng to this, number of Reviews doesnt appear correlated to price at all! Then again, this matrix is kind of small and hard to read. We need to play with the data more to find out what's going on.
